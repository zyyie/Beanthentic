Phase 1: Pre-Application Groundwork

Establish the foundation for your GI application

1. Identify Qualifying Product

Confirm product has quality/reputation attributable to Lipa City's geographic origin (e.g., Lipa Barako coffee, etc)

Product Documentation

Upload product details, photos, specifications

2. Establish Applicant Entity

Organize qualified applicant: producers' organization, LGU, or indigenous community group

Entity Documentation

Upload organization documents, certificates, bylaws

3. Stakeholder Consultations

Gather producers, LGU reps, industry groups; build consensus and governance

Consultation Records

Upload meeting minutes, attendance sheets, consensus documents

Phase 2: Preparing Application Documents

Compile all required documentation for filing

4. Manual of Specifications (MOP)

Draft product defining characteristics, rules, and standards for GI seal usage

MOP Documentation

Upload draft Manual of Specifications

5. Government Certification

Obtain certification validating causal link between product quality and geographic origin

Certification Documents

Upload government certifications, technical information

6. Application Details

Prepare applicant details, GI name, geographical area description, supporting documentation

Application Package

Upload completed application forms, maps, technical docs

Phase 3: Filing with IPOPHL

Submit your application to the Intellectual Property Office

7. File Application

Submit complete application to IPOPHL Bureau of Trademarks

Filing Documents

Upload final application package, cover letter, attachments

8. Pay Filing Fees

Complete payment of required IPOPHL filing fees

Payment Documentation

Upload payment receipts, fee schedules, proof of payment

Phase 4: Examination and Publication

Application review and public notice period

9. Formality & Substantive Examination

Application undergoes examination for registrability following IP Code provisions

Examination Documents

Upload examination reports, responses, clarifications

10. Respond to Deficiency Notices

Address any deficiencies within specified timeframe (typically 2 months)

Response Documents

Upload deficiency notices, response letters, extensions

11. Publication for Opposition

Application published for third-party observations/objections (1 month period)

Publication Documents

Upload publication notices, opposition filings, responses

Phase 5: Registration and Ongoing Compliance

Final registration and maintenance requirements

12. GI Registration Certificate

Receive official GI registration certificate from IPOPHL

Registration Documents

Upload registration certificate, official notices

13. Maintain Standards & Quality Control

Ongoing compliance: maintain product standards, quality control, prevent unauthorized use

Compliance Documentation

Upload quality control reports, compliance audits, monitoring records
